1989 - 1992

San Francisco, CA

I was born on February 7, 1989. My family lived in Glen Park. Here's my Dad and I visiting the house I grew up in, twenty-some years later.

My dad was a lawyer and my mom was getting her PhD. My sister Emma was born in 1992.

Fun Fact: I lived through the San Francisco earthquake of 1989 (and it happened on my Dad's birthday).

1992 - 2007

Bethesda, MD

My family moved to Bethesda, Maryland, a suburb of Washington D.C.

I attended Walt Whitman High School, where I played baseball and was the Editor-in-Chief of the school newspaper.

Fun Fact: I am a huge fan of all DC sports team, including the Redskins, Wizards, Nationals and Capitals. Since moving to SF, I've made sure to take every opportunity I can to see my teams play.

2007 - 2011

Los Angeles, CA

I attended UCLA, in sunny Los Angeles, where I studied English.

At UCLA, I was a member of the Sigma Alpha Epsilon fraternity and was a mentor and workshop leader helping academically struggling undergraduates. I also loved attending basketball and football games (clearly).

Fun Fact: One of my favorite memories at UCLA was when our fraternity would decorate our house with lights for Christmas. We went all out!

2011 - 2013

Helena, AR

After college, I joined the Teach for America program. I was accepted into the Mississippi Delta corps.

I moved to Helena-West Helena, AR where I taught Social Studies for two years at KIPP Delta College Preparatory School. Here is when I received a mohawk (that was later dyed blonde) after our kids met their goal in a book drive.

Fun Fact: The best thing about Arkansas was the extremely low cost of living. My total rent for this three-bedroom house was $850/month!

2013 - Present

San Francisco, CA - round 2

After two years in Arkansas, I switched schools and moved to San Francisco to teach at KIPP Bayview Academy. Here's a photo from our class trip to visit Stanford, where we made a human pyramid.

After one year teaching in SF, I moved to Next Gen Personal Finance, a nonprofit organization that creates fantastic, free materials for educators to teach their students about personal finance. Here I am at a conference in Texas, with a real cowboy!

Fun Fact: I now live with my girlfriend Alexa and our puppy Sophie. Sophie is a now just over a year old and a very energetic and silly pup (although she looks pretty sour here).
